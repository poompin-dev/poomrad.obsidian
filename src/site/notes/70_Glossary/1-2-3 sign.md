## What it is:
Bilateral **hilar** and **right paratracheal** lymphadenopathy on **chest radiograph**.  
It’s a classic but not exclusive sign of **sarcoidosis**.

---
## Components:
1. **Right paratracheal** LN enlargement
2. **Right hilar** LN enlargement
3. **Left hilar** LN enlargement
→ All three are symmetrically enlarged and usually **non-tender**.

---
## Common Associations:
- **Sarcoidosis** (most classic — esp. Stage I)
- Occasionally seen in:
    - **[[Lymphoma (overview)\|Lymphoma]]**
    - **TB**
    - **Fungal infections**
    > But **less symmetrical**, or with systemic clues
---
## Imaging Context:
- **CXR**: Best seen here. CT will show bulky, discrete, non-necrotic nodes.
- **HRCT chest**: Also assess for **perilymphatic nodules** to support sarcoidosis.
