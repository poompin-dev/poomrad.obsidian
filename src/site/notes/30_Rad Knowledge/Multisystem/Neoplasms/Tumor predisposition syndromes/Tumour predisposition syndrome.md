> [!summary] Mental model
> Inherited mutations → high lifetime risk of specific tumours; radiologist often makes the *first* call by recognising patterns (multiplicity, bilaterality, “wrong age”, or weird tumour combinations).

## When to suspect from imaging
- Multiple / bilateral tumours in a single organ  
	- Bilateral RCCs, vestibular schwannomas, optic pathway gliomas, phaeos.
- Multifocal lesions across organs
	- CNS + endocrine tumours, CNS + renal + pancreatic, lung cysts + renal tumours.
- “Too young” for that tumour
	- Early-onset RCC, colon cancer, breast cancer, sarcoma, endocrine tumours.
- Unusual tumour type / histology
	- Clear cell RCCs in 20s, spinal ependymomas + schwannomas, subependymal giant cell astrocytoma.
- Recurrent tumours after minimal interval or strong family history in request form.

## Classic imaging patterns
- **[[30_Rad Knowledge/Multisystem/Neoplasms/Tumor predisposition syndromes/Neurofibromatosis type 1\|NF1]]** – Optic pathway gliomas, plexiform neurofibromas, sphenoid dysplasia, bowel neurofibromas.
- **[[30_Rad Knowledge/Multisystem/Neoplasms/Tumor predisposition syndromes/Neurofibromatosis type 2\|NF2]]** – Bilateral vestibular schwannomas, multiple meningiomas, spinal ependymomas.
- **[[30_Rad Knowledge/Multisystem/Neoplasms/Tumor predisposition syndromes/Tuberous Sclerosis Complex (TSC)\|TSC]]** – Cortical tubers, subependymal nodules/SEGAs, [[30_Rad Knowledge/GU/Kidney/Renal masses/Renal Angiomyolipoma\|renal AML]]s (multiple, bilateral), lung LAM.
- **[[30_Rad Knowledge/Multisystem/Neoplasms/Tumor predisposition syndromes/Von Hippel-Lindau (overview)\|VHL]]** – CNS/retinal haemangioblastomas, clear cell RCC (multifocal), pancreatic cysts + NETs, phaeos.
- **MEN1**: “3 Ps”: parathyroid hyperplasia, pancreatic/duodenal NETs (gastrinoma, insulinoma, others), pituitary adenoma; often multiple pancreatic NETs.
- **MEN2A**: Medullary thyroid carcinoma, [[30_Rad Knowledge/GU/Adrenal gland/Phaeochromocytoma - Paraganglioma\|phaeochromocytoma]], parathyroid hyperplasia; look for bilateral phaeos + thyroid nodules.
- **MEN2B**: Aggressive early medullary thyroid carcinoma, phaeochromocytoma, mucosal neuromas, marfanoid habitus, intestinal ganglioneuromatosis.
- **FAP / Gardner** (APC): Innumerable colorectal adenomatous polyps → CRC, periampullary/duodenal tumours, desmoid tumours, [[30_Rad Knowledge/MSK/Bone/Bony lesions/Sclerotic lesions/Osteoma\|osteoma]]s, epidermoid cysts, CHRPE; post-colectomy desmoids are big imaging issue.
- **Lynch / HNPCC** (MMR): “Not many polyps but lots of cancers” early right-sided CRC, endometrial/ovarian ca, upper GI, small bowel and urothelial/upper tract tumours.
- **BRCA1/2**: Early-onset, often bilateral/multifocal breast ca; ovarian high-grade serous ca; ↑ pancreatic (and prostate with BRCA2) – MRI/high-risk screening protocols.
- **Birt–Hogg–Dubé** – Basal lung cysts (lower > upper), chromophobe/oncocytic [[30_Rad Knowledge/GU/Kidney/Renal masses/Renal cell carcinoma\|RCC]]s.
- **Li–Fraumeni** – Multiple primary sarcomas, breast, brain, [[adrenocortical carcinoma \|adrenocortical carcinoma ]]in young patients.
- **[[30_Rad Knowledge/Multisystem/Neoplasms/Tumor predisposition syndromes/PTEN-related diseases\|PTEN-related diseases]]**: “tigroid” cerebellum, multinodular goitre/thyroid ca, multiple breast nodules, mixed GI polyps, [[30_Rad Knowledge/GU/Kidney/Renal masses/Renal cell carcinoma\|RCC]] ([[30_Rad Knowledge/Neuro/Brain/CNS Neoplasm/Dysplastic cerebellar gangliocytoma\|Lhermitte-Duclos disease]]).
- **Gorlin (nevoid BCC)**: Multiple odontogenic keratocysts, early desmoplastic [[30_Rad Knowledge/Ped/Pediatric neoplasm/Medulloblastoma\|medulloblastoma]], bifid ribs/vertebral anomalies, multiple BCCs.
- **[[30_Rad Knowledge/Multisystem/Neoplasms/Tumor predisposition syndromes/PHACES syndrome\|PHACES]]**: Large segmental facial haemangioma + posterior fossa malformation, cervico-cerebral arterial anomalies, aortic arch/coarctation, ocular + sternal defects.
- **Peutz–Jeghers**: Numerous small bowel [[31_Other knowledge/Pathology/Harmartoma\|hamartomatous]] polyps → intussusception, mucocutaneous pigmentation, ↑ pancreatic & GI cancer risk.
- **DICER1**: Pleuropulmonary blastoma (cystic/solid lung mass in child), multinodular goitre, ovarian Sertoli–Leydig tumour, cystic nephroma, pineoblastoma.
- **Carney complex**: Multiple/recurrent cardiac myxomas, adrenal nodular hyperplasia, [[30_Rad Knowledge/Neuro/Brain/Sellar region/Pituitary adenoma\|pituitary adenoma]], testicular large-cell calcifying Sertoli tumours, lentiginosis.
- **Hereditary paraganglioma–phaeo (SDHx)**: Multiple [[30_Rad Knowledge/Neuro/Head and neck/Paragangliomas of the head and neck\|head & neck paragangliomas]], bilateral/multifocal phaeos, extra-adrenal abdominal [[30_Rad Knowledge/GU/Adrenal gland/Phaeochromocytoma - Paraganglioma\|paraganglioma]]s, intense MIBG/PET uptake.
- **Hereditary retinoblastoma (RB1)**: Bilateral/multifocal calcified [[30_Rad Knowledge/Neuro/Head and neck/Orbit/Retinoblastoma\|retinoblastoma]]s in infancy, later [[30_Rad Knowledge/MSK/Bone/Bony lesions/Lytic lesions/Malignant/Osteosarcoma\|osteosarcoma]]/other sarcomas (especially in prior RT field).
- **Beckwith–Wiedemann (BWS)**: Macroglossia, omphalocele, visceromegaly/hemihypertrophy; screen kidneys and liver ([[30_Rad Knowledge/Ped/Pediatric neoplasm/Renal masses/Wilms tumour\|Wilms tumour]], hepatoblastoma; also [[30_Rad Knowledge/Ped/Pediatric neoplasm/Neuroblastoma\|neuroblastoma]], adrenal, pancreatoblastoma).
