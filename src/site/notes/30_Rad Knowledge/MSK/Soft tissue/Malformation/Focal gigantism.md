## Core idea
- Differentiate by: 
	- **Tissue type overgrown**: osseous, soft tissue, or both
    - **Presence of cutaneous manifestations**
- All entities with cutaneous change (may) have overgrowth of both osseous and soft tissue components.

### Hyperemia (prior to skeletal maturation)
- **Juvenile idiopathic arthritis (JIA)** 
	- Mainly around **knee and elbow** (large joints)
- **Hemophilic arthropathy**
	- Hemarthrosis → hyperemia → osseous overgrowth
	-  Affects:
		- Knee (femoral condyle)
		- Elbow (radial head)
		- Ankle (distal tibia)
- **Infection**
	- **Chronic osteomyelitis**
	- **Tuberculosis**
- **Post-traumatic (fracture)**
___
## Pearls
- **JIA & Hemophilic Arthropathy**:
    - Imaging findings can be similar: ballooned epiphyses, widened intercondylar notch
- **Phleboliths**: suggest **vascular lesions** (hemangioma, [[30_Rad Knowledge/Multisystem/Venolymphatic malformation/Arteriovenous malformation (MSK)\|AVM]], [[30_Rad Knowledge/MSK/Bone/Multiple lesions conditions/Maffucci syndrome\|Maffucci syndrome]])
- **Digits involved** → Think **[[30_Rad Knowledge/MSK/Soft tissue/Malformation/Macrodystrophia lipomatosa\|Macrodystrophia lipomatosa]]** or enchondromatosis (**Ollier/Maffucci**)
