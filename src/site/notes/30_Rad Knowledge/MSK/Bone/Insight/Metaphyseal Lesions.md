## Overview
- **Metaphysis = fastest growing area of bone, best blood supply**
- Predilection for **metastasis** and **infection** due to vascularity
- Most **cystic bone lesions** occur here
- Location is **not a strong discriminator**—many lesions overlap with other regions

## Pearls
- **Most “classic” bone tumors first form in the metaphysis** (except those with specific diaphyseal or epiphyseal preference)
- **Mets, infection, and cystic lesions** ([[30_Rad Knowledge/MSK/Bone/Bony lesions/Lytic lesions/Benign/Simple Bone Cyst\|Simple Bone Cyst]], [[30_Rad Knowledge/MSK/Bone/Bony lesions/Lytic lesions/Benign/Aneurysmal bone cyst\|ABC]]) = think metaphysis
- **[[30_Rad Knowledge/MSK/Bone/Bony lesions/Lytic lesions/Benign/Non-ossifying fibroma\|NOF]]** is classic for distal femur metaphysis in kids
- **Matrix, age, and margin** matter more than just location here

> **Metaphysis is the “common room” for most bone lesions—location alone is rarely diagnostic.**
